About

A designer shaped by language & curiosity.

Shimaa N. Abu Al-Qumboz

From translation to interaction design.

My approach to design is informed by my background in English Translation, where I developed a sensitivity to language, context, and interpretation. Studying translation trained me to listen carefully and understand how meaning shifts across structures and systems — an awareness that later shaped my interest in UX/UI design.

I was initially drawn to design through color and visual expression, but it quickly became a problem-solving practice. I now approach design as a research-driven and iterative process, focused on understanding how people interact with digital systems and how interfaces influence behavior and decision making.

My work centers on creating clear, human-centered interfaces that respect cognitive diversity and bridge communication gaps. I am particularly interested in the intersection of language, culture, and technology — where design can act as a mediator between people and complex systems.

01

Mobile App  ·  Mental Health  ·  12 Weeks

Tahrar

Role: UX/UI Designer

A mobile mental health application designed to support Arabic-speaking users in managing psychological well-being — addressing anxiety, depression, and work-related stress. The platform combines access to specialists, guided programs, educational podcasts, and interactive self-assessment tools.

Research revealed users often struggle to name their emotional states and hesitate to seek professional help due to stigma. The design response prioritized calm visual language, non-judgmental copy, and progressive disclosure of information.

Key Outcome: Users could more easily identify and reflect on their emotional states. The community feature reduced fear of judgment and encouraged open expression through anonymity controls.

02

Web Platform  ·  Humanitarian  ·  6 Weeks

Eta

Role: UX/UI Designer

ETA is a water distribution and contribution platform supporting humanitarian and health-focused organizations. The platform works within a multi-stakeholder ecosystem involving donors, partner organizations, and distribution teams.

The design process focused on clarity, structure, and ethical communication — structuring information to explain how contributions are allocated, presenting partner organizations to reinforce credibility, and prioritizing neutral language to avoid sensationalizing humanitarian work.

Key Outcome: The final design presents ETA as a trustworthy humanitarian platform. Users can clearly identify partner organizations and engage without cognitive overload.
✨
03

E-Commerce Platform  ·  Print-on-Demand  ·  6 Weeks

Infinity

Role: UX/UI Designer

A conceptual e-commerce platform focused on print-on-demand services, allowing users to create and order customized gifts, advertisements, and printed products. The platform bridges creativity and production through both manual design tools and AI-powered generation.

The design is organized around three core pillars: Create (generate or upload designs), Preview (visualize on realistic mockups), and Order (seamless checkout and tracking). AI generation was intentionally kept as an optional support tool to preserve creative control.

Key Outcome: The platform concept was well-received for its clarity, flexibility, and strong emphasis on visual confidence before purchase.
