🧠 01

Mobile App · Mental Health · Arabic-Speaking Users

Tahrar

Role: UX/UI Designer  ·  Duration: 12 Weeks

Context & Problem

Tahrar is a mobile mental health application designed to support users in managing psychological well-being, with a focus on conditions such as anxiety, depression, and work-related stress. The app targets Arabic-speaking users and aims to make mental health support more accessible, structured, and culturally sensitive.

The platform combines access to mental health specialists and doctors, guided programs, educational podcasts, and interactive self-assessment tools. It also encourages users to engage with their mental and physical states through daily emotional check-ins and personalized recommendations.

Design Question

How might we design a mental health application that helps users understand, track, and improve their psychological well-being while reducing stigma and supporting emotional awareness in an Arabic-speaking context?

Research Insights

Users often struggle to recognize and name their emotional states, feel overwhelmed by complex mental health information, and hesitate to seek professional help due to stigma. They expressed a preference for experiences that feel supportive rather than clinical, with gradual engagement instead of immediate commitment.

Design Response

Calm visual language Soft colors & clear spacing Guided mental health flows Non-judgmental language Progressive disclosure Optional mood check-ins

Reflection & Learnings

This project emphasized the role of safety, anonymity, and tone in mental health design. Allowing users to control their identity increased openness and trust within the platform. Testing was limited in scale, and long-term community behavior was not evaluated — the app is designed as a supportive system, not a substitute for professional care.

Key Outcome

Testing showed that users could more easily identify and reflect on their emotional states. Mental health measures were perceived as supportive rather than diagnostic. The community feature reduced fear of judgment and encouraged open expression through chosen characters and anonymity controls.

💧 02

Web Platform · Humanitarian · Multi-Stakeholder

Eta

Role: UX/UI Designer  ·  Duration: 6 Weeks

Context & Problem

ETA is a water distribution and contribution platform that supports humanitarian and health-focused organizations by facilitating water delivery, donations, and coordination with partner entities. The platform works in collaboration with international and regional organizations focused on human health and emergency response.

The project operates within a multi-stakeholder ecosystem involving donors, partner organizations, and distribution teams. The core challenge was to communicate trust, credibility, and impact while making complex humanitarian processes understandable to a broad audience.

Design Challenge

How might we design a platform that supports transparent water distribution and humanitarian collaboration while remaining clear, credible, and accessible to diverse stakeholders?

Process & Decisions

Structured contribution flow Partner legitimacy signals Reduced contribution friction Neutral language & visuals Information architecture focus

Reflection

This project strengthened the ability to design for systems rather than individuals, and to consider ethics, trust, and responsibility as core UX concerns. Future exploration could include impact visualization, real-time distribution tracking, and scalable partnership frameworks.

Key Outcome

The final design presents ETA as a trustworthy and well-structured humanitarian platform, emphasizing transparency and ease of contribution. Users can clearly identify partner organizations, understand the flow of water distribution, and engage with the platform without cognitive overload.

✨ 03

E-Commerce · Print-on-Demand · AI-Assisted Design

Infinity

Role: UX/UI Designer  ·  Duration: 6 Weeks

Context & Problem

Infinity is a conceptual e-commerce platform focused on print-on-demand services, allowing users to create and order customized gifts, advertisements, and printed products. The platform bridges creativity and production by enabling users to transform ideas into physical items through both manual design tools and AI-powered generation.

Unlike traditional e-commerce platforms, Infinity emphasizes co-creation, personalization, and experimentation — making design accessible even to users without creative or technical skills.

Conceptual Framework

The design is organized around three core pillars:

🎨 Create — manual tools or AI generation 👁 Preview — realistic product mockups 📦 Order — seamless checkout & tracking

Visual Language

Clean modular layout Neutral base colors Bold accent colors Real-time mockups Clear pricing feedback

Key Design Decision

AI generation was intentionally kept as an optional support tool rather than the default entry point — ensuring users retained creative control while reducing friction for those starting without a clear idea.

Key Outcome

Infinity was designed as a high-fidelity e-commerce platform focused on print-on-demand products. The platform concept was well received for its clarity, flexibility, and strong emphasis on visual confidence before purchase. The complete user journey covers idea generation through checkout and order management.
